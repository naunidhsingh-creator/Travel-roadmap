# The Explorer's Path — Travel Career Roadmap

A visual career roadmap for anyone who wants to build a life around travel — not as a tourist, but as a fixer, writer, and adventurer.

🌍 **Live site:** [olive-johna-46.tiiny.site](https://olive-johna-46.tiiny.site)

---

## What this is

I'm a 9th grade student from India with one clear goal — explore the world, stay free, and build a career that makes travel the work itself, not a reward at the end of it.

This roadmap maps out everything I've researched and planned:

- The career combination of **Fixer + Travel Writer** and why they work together
- The **8 soft skills** needed to build this life
- The **regions** worth going deep in
- A **year-by-year timeline** from school to independence
- The **mentors** whose work guides this path
- The **6 books** every aspiring travel writer and fixer should read

---

## Who this is for

- Students who want to build a career around travel but don't know where to start
- Anyone drawn to adventure, exploration, and human connection
- People who have zero interest in photography or content creation but still want travel at the centre of their work

---

## Career path covered

| Career | What it is |
|--------|-----------|
| Fixer | On-ground expert hired by journalists, filmmakers & executives |
| Travel Writer | Translate places and people into words — magazines, books, essays |
| Tour Designer | Architect experiences and journeys for travelers |
| Travel Consultant | Plan and advise on trips for clients |
| Cultural Researcher | Study cultures on the ground for NGOs, universities, think tanks |

---

## Mentors featured

- **Rolf Potts** — Primary mentor. Author of Vagabonding. The closest living model to this lifestyle.
- **Paul Theroux** — Travel writing craft and honest observation
- **Robert Young Pelton** — Fixing and operating in complex regions

---

## Books in reading order

1. *Vagabonding* — Rolf Potts
2. *The Great Railway Bazaar* — Paul Theroux
3. *Marco Polo Didn't Go There* — Rolf Potts
4. *Maximum City* — Suketu Mehta
5. *The Journalist and the Murderer* — Janet Malcolm
6. *The World's Most Dangerous Places* — Robert Young Pelton
