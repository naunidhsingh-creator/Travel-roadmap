Career Roadmap — Naunidh

The Explorer's Path

Fixer · Travel Writer · Adventurer — built for freedom, fuelled by curiosity

North star
✦

Explore the world. Stay free. Live the adventure.

Not a job that allows travel — a life where travel, writing, and deep cultural immersion are the work itself. Freedom and adventure are non-negotiable. Everything else is built around them.

Career combination
Career one

Fixer

On-ground expert hired by journalists, filmmakers & executives. You navigate logistics, make local connections, and translate culture. Deep regional knowledge. No camera. High demand.

+
Career two

Travel Writer

Translate places and people into words. Observe, talk to locals, dig into culture, write. Published in magazines, books, or your own platform. Voice and insight over credentials.

Why they reinforce each other
01

Fixing funds the freedom

Immediate cash from fixing clients lets you keep moving. No desk. No fixed location. Income follows you.

02

Writing builds reputation

Published work becomes your portfolio. It attracts better fixing clients and opens doors no CV ever could.

03

Access creates content

As a fixer you're inside the story. You see what tourists and journalists never see. That's your writing material.

Soft skills to build
👁
Deep observation
Notice small specific details — not grand scenery, but a vendor's hands, a smell, a silence
🗣
Human connection
Talk to strangers everywhere. Build trust quickly across cultures and languages
🧭
Navigation & logistics
Move through difficult terrain, plan routes, manage uncertainty without panic
✍️
Writing craft
Translate experience into words others feel. Voice matters more than grammar
🌐
Languages
A fixer without the local language is half a fixer. Start one language now, go deep
🤝
Network building
Local contacts are your greatest asset. Collect relationships, not business cards
⚡
Adaptability
Plans always change in the field. Stay calm, find solutions fast, never freeze
📖
Cultural literacy
Understand histories, religions, politics of places before you arrive. Context is everything
Regions worth going deep in

Pick one region. Go deep. Become the expert.

The most powerful fixers and travel writers aren't generalists — they own a region. They know its people, politics, hidden corners, and real stories. Depth over breadth, at least at the start. The best fixer markets are places with high global interest but genuine complexity — where outsiders struggle to navigate alone.

Year by year roadmap
Now
Gr. 9

Plant the seeds

Read voraciously — start with Vagabonding. Begin one language seriously. Travel within India with real curiosity. Start a private travel journal. Observe everything.

Gr. 10–12

Sharpen the instincts

More ambitious travel — neighbouring countries, diverse regions. Continue language seriously. Write publicly in small ways — a blog, school publications. Follow Rolf Potts' podcast Deviate weekly.

College

Enter the industry

Study international relations, journalism, or tourism. First trips to your target region. Assist an experienced fixer. Pitch and publish in real outlets. Build your first local contacts.

Early 20s

Go independent

First solo fixing jobs. First serious published pieces. Deep immersion in chosen region. Begin writing your first book. Let the reputation build through quality, not volume.

Mid 20s

Own your niche

Known in your region. Work comes to you. Book published or in progress. Freedom and adventure are no longer the goal — they are simply your life.

Guiding lights — mentors
RP
Rolf Potts
Travel Writer & Philosopher
The closest living model to the life you want. Built freedom and adventure through writing, not fame. Accessible via podcast Deviate and rolfpotts.com. Start here.
PT
Paul Theroux
Travel Writer & Author
Brutal honesty. Never romanticises. Teaches you to see places as they are, not as you wish them to be. Read him to sharpen your writing craft.
RY
Robert Young Pelton
Fixer & Conflict Journalist
Decades in the world's most complex regions. If the fixer path calls you most, his work is the raw, unfiltered manual for that life.
Books — your curriculum
Vagabonding
Rolf Potts
The manual for building a life around travel. Freedom as a foundation, not a reward. Read this first.
Read first
The Great Railway Bazaar
Paul Theroux
Classic travel writing. Shows how to observe cultures honestly and translate journeys into narrative.
Read second
Marco Polo Didn't Go There
Rolf Potts
Essay collection. See Potts' craft up close — how he thinks about places, people, and the act of travel itself.
Read third
Maximum City
Suketu Mehta
Deep cultural immersion in Mumbai. Shows how travel writing and cultural inquiry can produce something genuinely important.
Read fourth
The Journalist and the Murderer
Janet Malcolm
Teaches you how the best fixers and journalists actually think. Essential for understanding the craft behind the adventure.
Read fifth
The World's Most Dangerous Places
Robert Young Pelton
Raw and practical. The fixer's field guide to operating in complex, difficult, and extraordinary regions of the world.
Read when ready
